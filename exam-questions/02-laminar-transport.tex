\subsubsection{In steady state combustion studies, enthalpy $h$ is often chosen as the energy variable. What is the relation between enthalpy and temperature? Explain the physical meaning of the different terms in the equation for enthalpy (equation will be given). Explain the physical meaning of the different terms in the equation for temperature (equation will be given).}

{\color{gray}\footnotesize [Claude context: check Lecture 8 (Laminar and Turbulent Transport Processes -- conservation equations, energy/enthalpy formulation); Kuo Ch.3 (sec.\ 1-2, 4, 11, conservation equations for reacting flows); Maas notes Ch.3 / Warnatz Ch.3 \& Ch.12]}

\paragraph{Answer}


Enthalpy is a measure of internal energy; temperature is one of the forms in which internal energy is expressed. Total temperature is related to enthalpy through $c_p$, which also corresponds to enthalpy for isobaric or constant-volume systems.

Enthalpy considers internal energy, kinetic energy, and pressure.

The conservation equation considers the change in time, density variation/transport, as well as convective flux, diffusive heat transfer, fluid stress, the Dufour effect, and the gradient in species mass concentration.
